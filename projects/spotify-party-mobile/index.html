Spotify Party Mobile
Going mobile
Spotify Party was great! However, there was one major issue... Who actually listens to music on a computer? Most people listen to music from their phones. It's way more convenient and accessible.
Cross-platform
I decided to take use React Native rather than using Java (like I usually do for mobile) so I can also develop an iOS app, which I've never done before. React Native allowed me to do both at the same time.
First steps
This was my first iOS compatible and cross-platform app. A lot of the same aspects from the desktop version carried on here, but I had to use a completely different method for monitoring and controlling playback.

Music. Together. Now on mobile.
Now you can listen to music with your friends no matter where you are with your phone. Pause and play at your will and your friends will follow along. No matter where you are in the world, there's never an excuse to sit together and listen to music!
Spotify Integration
The best part? It's strongly integrated with Spotify. All your listening activity will still be recorded by Spotify, you can still control playback from any device and you're automatically logged in using your Spotify account details. User playback is retrieved via polling rather than the handy Web Player SDK that's available on the web.
